## 1. RAG-driven Multi-Agent LLM Framework with Task Decomposition for Beyond 5G Auto-Configuration
**Authors:** İrşat Emin Sarıdaş, Onur Salan, Ali Görçin, et al.

**Summary:** This paper addresses hallucination and structural-inconsistency failures of single-LLM pipelines on multi-step network-configuration work by splitting the job across specialized agents — generation, closed-loop verification, and deployment — each of which is grounded by a shared semantic-retrieval pipeline keyed off the relevant 3GPP standards and vendor manuals. The framework couples that retrieval substrate with explicit task decomposition so that no individual agent has to reason about the full configuration at once, and standards-aligned evidence is injected at the point of each sub-decision. The contribution is methods-and-architecture: an opinionated multi-agent topology (role split + RAG grounding + closed-loop verification) for a domain where outputs must be checkable against external technical specifications.

**Theme:** 2 — Multi-agent frameworks & orchestration (role assignment, RAG-grounded coordination, task decomposition).

**Relevance:** The strongest in-window multi-agent paper: it makes the orchestration choices (specialized roles, retrieval-grounded coordination, closed-loop verification) explicit and ties them to a checkable external spec — a direct method contribution to Theme 2, not a survey or position piece.

**Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.01222

## 2. A Communication-Centric 6G-LLM Architecture for Scalable Tactical Autonomous Defense Vehicle Networks
**Authors:** Kiran Khurshid, Shumaila Javaid, Nasir Saeed

**Summary:** This work proposes a hierarchical multi-agent architecture for fleets of autonomous defense vehicles in which **edge-assisted LLM reasoning** is co-designed with a 6G-style semantic-communication substrate, so that what each agent has to *say* to its peers is shaped by what reasoning the LLM tier needs to perform. Coordination is structured around the communication bottleneck rather than around a single planner, with the explicit goals of reducing per-vehicle communication overhead, controlling latency tails as fleet size grows, and keeping mission success robust under congestion. The reported scaling result at 30 vehicles is a 75.2% latency reduction, +68.7 pp mission-success improvement, and 88.6% communication-overhead reduction relative to a 5G-style baseline — a concrete demonstration that placing the orchestration layer at the comms boundary, not the planner, can change the scaling behaviour of an LLM-driven multi-agent system.

**Theme:** 2 — Multi-agent frameworks & orchestration (architecture; edge-LLM coordination; communication-aware multi-agent design).

**Relevance:** A second in-window multi-agent contribution that takes a structurally different stance from #1 — orchestration co-designed with the communication channel, instead of with retrieval grounding — and shows large scaling-curve effects rather than just qualitative wins. Useful as a methodological contrast to #1 and squarely on Theme 2.

**Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.01312

## 3. PolySpeech-100: A Large-Scale Benchmark for Speech Understanding Across 100+ Languages and Dialects
**Authors:** Sicheng Yang, Shulan Ruan, Shiwei Wu, et al.

**Summary:** PolySpeech-100 is a 110-variant evaluation suite for end-to-end Speech-LLMs that pushes past pure ASR by targeting semantic comprehension and dialect breadth, deliberately covering 19 Chinese dialects and 80+ low-resource languages that current benchmarks under-serve. The authors use a hybrid construction pipeline that pairs gold-standard human recordings with instruction-driven synthetic speech to make coverage at this scale tractable. Empirically, across 22 state-of-the-art systems (incl. Gemini-3, GPT-Audio, Qwen2.5-Omni) the headline finding is that open-source end-to-end Speech-LLMs *outperform* cascade systems on heavy dialects, and chain-of-thought prompting tends to *degrade* speech understanding for most models — a methodological flag for anyone porting CoT-style scaffolding to the speech modality.

**Theme:** 3 — Core LLM advances (multilingual/multi-dialect Speech-LLM evaluation; CoT-on-speech finding).

**Relevance:** Included as the most theme-relevant in-window paper outside of Theme 2 — a serious 22-model, 110-variant evaluation that yields a transferable negative result about CoT on speech inputs. Relevance is **narrower** than typical Theme 3 papers in prior digests (it isn't about agentic-system evaluation, reasoning, or PEFT for agents per se), so treat this as a borderline pick to surface a fresh empirical finding rather than as a top-priority read.

**Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.01016
