## 1. PolyGnosis 2.0: Enhancing LLM Reasoning via Agentic Harness Engineering for Polymarket and OSINT Insight Extraction
**Authors:** Daren Wang, Hong Xu, Jiawen Xian

**Summary:** PolyGnosis 2.0 is a multi-agent "agentic harness" that fuses prediction-market signals (Polymarket anomalies) with global open-source intelligence streams (the GDELT events/tone database) to surface what the authors call "Perspective Mismatches" — divergences between market-implied sentiment and the global media narrative — and treats those mismatches as high-alpha trading signals. Rather than proposing a new model, the work isolates and measures the contribution of individual "harness engineering" techniques layered around the LLM — reflection loops, tool-calling, divide-and-conquer task partitioning, and chain-of-thought — within a deliberately high-noise financial/OSINT setting. The paper's contribution is the architecture plus an empirical quantification of how much each harnessing technique improves reasoning-driven insight extraction in this domain.

**Theme:** Multi-agent frameworks & orchestration (theme 2), with a core-LLM agentic-reasoning angle (theme 3).

**Relevance:** The only paper with a submission date (May 25, 2026) verifiable inside the 48h window; it is a multi-agent agentic system whose central question — how orchestration/harness choices change LLM reasoning quality — sits squarely in themes 2 and 3.

**Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.25958
