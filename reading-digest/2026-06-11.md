# Reading digest — 2026-06-11

## 1. Toward Generalist Autonomous Research via Hypothesis-Tree Refinement
- **Authors**: Jiajie Jin, Yuyang Hu, Kai Qiu, et al.
- **Summary**: Introduces Arbor, an autonomous-research framework that pairs a long-lived coordinator with short-lived executors and a persistent Hypothesis Tree Refinement (HTR) structure linking hypotheses, artifacts, evidence, and distilled insights across many experiments. The HTR acts as a cumulative episodic memory that propagates reusable lessons and refines the search frontier between runs, while executors test individual hypotheses in isolated worktrees. On six real research tasks and MLE-Bench Lite, Arbor outperforms Codex and Claude Code by more than 2.5x average held-out gain and reaches 86.36% Any Medal with GPT-5.5.
- **Theme**: Memory + Multi-agent orchestration
- **Relevance**: Cleanly combines two of the three priority themes — a persistent cross-time memory structure *and* a coordinator/executor multi-agent architecture — with strong empirical results against well-known baselines.
- **Link**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11926

## 2. InternVideo3: Agentify Foundation Models with Multimodal Contextual Reasoning
- **Authors**: Ziang Yan, Sheng Xia, Jiashuo Yu, et al.
- **Summary**: Frames long-video understanding as a closed-loop agentic process under a shared, evolving context that bundles observations, instructions, reasoning steps, tool actions, and memory (called Multimodal Contextual Reasoning). To keep this context tractable, the authors introduce M^2LA, a token-preserving reparameterization that compresses KV-cache state while keeping the full token stream available, and train the model through continued pretraining, short-to-long SFT, rule-based RL, and on-policy distillation. The resulting agent reports strong results on Video-MME, MLVU, and EgoSchema and behaves as a retrieval-tool video agent.
- **Theme**: Memory for LLM agents (context/state management) + Core LLM advance
- **Relevance**: Directly tackles long-horizon agent state and KV/context management — exactly the working-memory / context-management thread in priority (1).
- **Link**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.12195

## 3. Role-Agent: Bootstrapping LLM Agents via Dual-Role Evolution
- **Authors**: Xucong Wang, Ziyu Ma, Shidong Yang, et al.
- **Summary**: Proposes a single LLM that bootstraps itself by alternately taking the role of agent and environment, generating its own targeted practice trajectories and environment-aware reasoning signals. The dual-role co-evolution loop produces a curriculum without external task supplies and improves both planning quality and downstream task success. The result is a self-play-style recipe specifically tailored to agent capability acquisition.
- **Theme**: Multi-agent frameworks (role/tool assignment, self-play roles)
- **Relevance**: Novel take on role assignment inside an agentic loop — relevant to priority (2) on coordination and role architectures.
- **Link**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.10917

## 4. Agentic Environment Engineering for Large Language Models: A Survey
- **Authors**: Jiachun Li, Zhuoran Jin, Tianyi Men, et al.
- **Summary**: A structured survey of the agent-environment lifecycle covering environment modeling, synthesis (symbolic vs. neural), evaluation, and application. It explicitly maps four agent-evolution pathways — memory-centric experience evolution, orchestration-centric workflow evolution, trajectory-centric offline evolution, and exploration-centric online evolution — and flags Multi-agent Environments and Environment-as-a-Service as open frontiers. Useful as a taxonomy when comparing memory-centric vs. orchestration-centric multi-agent designs.
- **Theme**: Multi-agent frameworks & orchestration (survey)
- **Relevance**: One of the few surveys explicitly organizing the field around memory and orchestration axes that align with priorities (1) and (2). Survey, but well-scoped and recent.
- **Link**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.12191

## 5. TRACE: A Unified Rollout Budget Allocation Framework for Efficient Agentic Reinforcement Learning
- **Authors**: Heming Zou, Qi Wang, Yun Qu, et al.
- **Summary**: Targets the low-reward-contrast problem in multi-turn agentic RL by modeling each ReAct-style thought-action-observation turn as a node and forming tree-structured rollouts whose budget is allocated to prefixes most likely to flip terminal reward. A shared, generalizable predictor estimates conditional success probability at these anchors to drive the allocation. On agentic benchmarks the method beats competitive baselines at equal sampling cost, e.g. +2.8 points Multi-Hop QA average on Qwen3-14B.
- **Theme**: Multi-agent / agentic-system training (rollout structure)
- **Relevance**: Concrete training-time mechanism for multi-turn agent rollouts; relevant to priority (2) and the agentic-eval thread in priority (3).
- **Link**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11119

## 6. Claw-SWE-Bench: A Benchmark for Evaluating OpenClaw-style Agent Harnesses on Coding Tasks
- **Authors**: Mengyu Zheng, Kai Han, Boxun Li, et al.
- **Summary**: Introduces a SWE-bench-style benchmark and adapter protocol that makes heterogeneous "claw" agent harnesses comparable under fixed prompt, runtime budget, workspace contract, and evaluator, with 350 multilingual issues across 43 repos and an 80-instance Lite subset. A minimal direct-diff adapter scores 19.1% Pass@1 with OpenClaw, while a full adapter reaches 73.4% on the same GLM 5.1 backbone — model choice swings Pass@1 by 29.4 pp and harness choice by 27.4 pp under fixed models. Promotes harness design and cost accounting to first-class axes of evaluation.
- **Theme**: Multi-agent / agentic-system evaluation
- **Relevance**: Adds rigor to comparing agent harnesses — directly addresses the agentic-system evaluation thread in priority (3).
- **Link**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.12344

## 7. Workflow-GYM: Long-Horizon Evaluation of Computer-use Agentic Tasks in Real-World Professional Fields
- **Authors**: Liya Zhu, Jingzhe Ding, Jian Zhang, et al.
- **Summary**: Proposes a benchmark of long-horizon, professional GUI workflows (computer-use tasks in real software) and shows current agents struggle with workflow-consistency and domain-specific software understanding, achieving low end-to-end success. Quantifies where computer-use agents break in extended workflows rather than isolated steps. Provides a yardstick for the orchestration/long-horizon side of agent design.
- **Theme**: Multi-agent / computer-use agent evaluation
- **Relevance**: Useful empirical pressure-test for orchestration and long-horizon coordination claims in priority (2).
- **Link**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11042

## 8. EEVEE: Test-time Prompt Learning in the Real World for Self-Improving Agents
- **Authors**: Weixian Xu, Shilong Liu, Mengdi Wang
- **Summary**: Targets the realistic setting where an agent faces heterogeneous, mixed data streams at test time. EEVEE clusters incoming tasks and co-evolves a router with task-specific prompts so the agent can specialize on the fly without parameter updates. Demonstrates self-improvement during deployment via prompt-level rather than weight-level adaptation.
- **Theme**: Multi-agent / agent architectures (test-time adaptation)
- **Relevance**: Touches the orchestration thread — routing across specialized prompt experts — and a lightweight memory-of-tasks mechanism relevant to (1) and (2).
- **Link**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11182

## 9. Reason, Then Re-reason: Cross-view Revisiting Improves Spatial Reasoning
- **Authors**: Chaofan Ma, Zhenjie Mao, Yuhuan Yang, et al.
- **Summary**: A training-free inference-time framework where an MLLM first forms a spatial hypothesis from an egocentric video, then verifies or revises it against a synthesized novel-view video generated by a Geometry-to-Video pipeline. Treats reasoning as revisitable rather than single-shot, letting the model amend conclusions when complementary evidence appears. Reports that open-source MLLMs rival proprietary SOTA on VSI-Bench and STI-Bench under this protocol.
- **Theme**: Core LLM advance (reasoning / planning)
- **Relevance**: Embodies a revise-when-new-evidence-arrives reasoning loop that is structurally relevant to agentic reasoning under partial observability.
- **Link**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.11683

## 10. ComBench: Rigorous Proof Reasoning and Constructive Realization in Olympiad-Level Combinatorics
- **Authors**: Shunkai Zhang, Haoran Zhang, Yun Luo, et al.
- **Summary**: A 100-problem Olympiad-grade combinatorics benchmark organized around analysis-centric (proof) and construction-centric (explicit constructive) tasks, scored with rubric-guided proof grading plus deterministic construction verification. Shows ComBench is far from saturated — strongest model hits 65.4% Avg / 75.3% Best@4 — and that rigorous proof reasoning and constructive realization are distinct, separately rankable capabilities. Useful as a diagnostic for the creative side of mathematical reasoning.
- **Theme**: Core LLM advance (reasoning evaluation)
- **Relevance**: Carves out a sharp evaluation of proof vs. construction reasoning, complementing the agentic evaluation work in priority (3).
- **Link**: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.10479
